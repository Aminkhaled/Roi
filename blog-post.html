Seven Best ROI Practices to Remember While When Measuring Training ROI

Just as essential training is for the employees it is equally important for the organization to have a sustained knowledge of the training program's success.

Several reports and statistics surrounding training ROI, have reported that more and more companies are investing money or allocating more resources to measure the effectiveness or impact of a training program.

Measuring Training ROI is important as it justifies the success of the training and the future training budgets.

That is why measuring training ROI using the ROI Best practices not only helps the organization, allocates even more resources but also helps employees increase their capacity to adaption new methods and technologies.

Best ROI Practices to remember while When Measuring Training ROI 1. Research before you start your training Always conduct an initial analysis and set objectives for your training. Effective training will provide a good opportunity for every employee, it will help employees to grow their knowledge, improve their job skills and become more efficient in the respective workplace.

Therefore, the training must cover product knowledge, service knowledge, character growth, new trends, subject of upcoming trends, and much more. With ROI Methodology® you will be able to research each stage and set perfect training goals you’re your organization.

2. Program objectives should be agreed upon while planning for the program Objectives are statements describing the results to be achieved, and how they will be achieved. You usually need multiple objectives to address a single goal. Objectives are essential as it helps provide program clarity and strong links to evaluation. It is much easier to evaluate a program with SMART Objectives. ROI Methodology® suggests setting objectives according to your need Analysis. Levels involved in setting objectives Reaction Objectives, Learning Objectives, Application Objectives, Impact objectives, and ROI Objectives.

3. Measurement is Done in 5 levels Reaction and Planned Action: Measures participant satisfaction with the program/project and captures planned action. Learning: Measures changes in knowledge and skills Application: Measures changes in on-the-job behaviour or actions Business Impact: Captures changes in business impact measures Return On Investment: Compares monetary benefits to the costs

4. Consult and Stay Consistent With Existing KPIs Use the existing key performance indicators (KPIs) of your company like the sales, marketing, and revenue figures, bugs, data breaches, customer complaints, and much more. When it comes to KPI do not overwhelm yourself by tracking more than what you need. For each goal, use only a few KPI, and each should correlate with your goal.

5. Establish Benchmarks for Pre-training Comparison There are two common types of benchmarks internal and external. Internal benchmarks allow L&D professionals to effectively compare the training details across the organization’s curriculum and over consecutive periods. External benchmarks allow L&D professionals to compare their training effectiveness data to broader industry standards. It is a good idea to establish benchmarks associated with a specific learning model and compare course scores to benchmark learning values.

6. Understand the training requirements through data collection and feedback Use only credible sources, user surveys, and questionnaires, Facilitation assessments, Written tests and exercises, Skill practices, Performance demonstrations, Simulations, Team assessments to get answers to your fundamental questions. Using these data makes getting feedback easier, and helps organizations to measure the Training ROI effectively.

7. Quality evaluation deliver positive results Quality evaluation of the training is necessary to understand whether the training has achieved its objectives. Using appropriate evaluation tools improves the outcome of the training and gives positive results. Evaluation of the training can be done using the Phillips ROI Methodology. ROI Methodology® is one of the ultimate training measuring and evaluation tools, which not only properly create the training goals, set objectives but also helps organizations to measure and evaluate the results of the training. Use the above-mentioned ROI practices for effective training ROI.
